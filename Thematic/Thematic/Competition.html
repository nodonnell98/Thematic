Competitions

Empire Mock Trial World Championships
New York, 2016

In 2016, I participated in the mock trial world championships as captain of the UK team. This involved presenting a court case with attorneys and witnesses against an opposing team’s case. By providing evidence, questioning the opposing witnesses and delivering opening/closing speeches the team displays it's case to the judge and jury.

As captain, I had the responsibility of motivating my team, attending captain's meetings and being an example for my teammates to follow. In order to do this, my skills as a leader and public speaker were tested. Conducting a closing speech requires the ability to take the information discussed in court and improvise a clear, persuasive argument on the spot that must last at least seven minutes - I did this on an international level and consistently scored 10/10s for it.

This competition was a crucible for my ability to think calmly, communicate effectivley, work in a team and solve problems on the fly.

FLUX Business Competition

Strategic planning and professional pitching were the aim of this competition.
In a team of six, we had to develop a marketing strategy for Sponge - an e-learning company - looking to to attract and hire the top 10% of university graduates. This competition developed new skills for me such as strategic business planning and networking, as I was able to talk to the experts on the judging panel afterwards.

Despite not making it to the final, our team were approached by the Chief of Staff at Sponge, as well as other judges explaing that they were impressed with our idea and the pitch we delivered. Knowing that we produced a strategy that was approved of by the company we were developing for was the highlight of the competition and boosted my confidence in the skills I had developed.
